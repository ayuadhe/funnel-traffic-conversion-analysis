Between January and September 2025, the platform recorded 5,680 sessions, but only 782 resulted in conversions. This gap indicates bottlenecks within the customer journey that limit optimal growth in the conversion rate.

This analysis aims to: **identify leakage points across the funnel, evaluate traffic source effectiveness, and assess device performance.** The findings provide **actionable insights to prioritize optimization efforts in areas with the highest potential** to increase conversions.


### Business Questions:
1. Where does the most significant drop-off occur within the customer journey?
2. Which traffic source generates the highest conversion efficiency?
3. How does each device perform in terms of engagement and conversion?
4. Which optimization areas should be prioritized to maximize conversion uplift?

<br>
<h1 align="center">Key Findings</h1>
<br>

1. **The primary bottleneck occurs at the Product Page → Add to Cart transition**, with the highest drop-off rate (57%).
2. **Users who reach Checkout demonstrate strong purchase intent**, with a 91% step conversion rate to confirmation.
3. **Social Media drives the highest traffic volume (37.5%) but delivers relatively lower conversion efficiency** compared to Email and Direct channels.
4. **Mobile dominates total traffic (52.8%)**, making it the highest-impact device for optimization.
5. **Tablet significantly underperforms across engagement and conversion metrics**, indicating substantial UX-related friction
6. **Engagement alone does not guarantee conversion**. The gap between product exploration and purchase completion signals evaluation-stage inefficiencies.

<h1 align="center">Deep Dive</h1>

### 1. Customer Journey & Drop-off Analysis

<br> 

<p align="center">
  <img width="702" height="108" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d7f4dfe9-b928-4529-988c-43357b2adfe5" />
</p>

Out of 5,680 sessions, only 782 converted, resulting in a 13.8% conversion rate. Users spent an average of 176 seconds per session and added around 4 items to their cart, showing active product exploration.
Despite this engagement, conversions remain limited, indicating mid-funnel leakage that requires further analysis.

<br>

<p align="center">
  <img width="478" height="254" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6b1839db-a654-465f-a16c-d7ad493e6fd0" />
</p>

The funnel shows a disproportionate drop-off between stages:

- 5.7K sessions start at the Homepage.
- Only 65% proceed to Product View.
- This figure declines sharply to 28% at Add to Cart.

The steep drop between Product View and Add to Cart highlights significant friction in moving users from product discovery to purchase intent. In contrast, the decline from Checkout (20%) to Purchase (18%) is relatively gradual, indicating that the main bottleneck is not payment execution but the product evaluation stage, where persuasion appears insufficient to drive cart commitment.
<br>

The breakdown table reinforces the identified bottleneck.

- **Product Page → Cart** records the highest drop-off at 57% (2,097 sessions).
- **Checkout → Confirmation** shows a 91% step conversion rate, indicating strong purchase commitment among users who reach checkout.

This pattern confirms that once purchase intent is formed, users are highly likely to complete the transaction. Consequently, optimization efforts should focus upstream, particularly on improving the conversion from Product Page to Add to Cart by:

- Enhancing UI/UX
- Clarifying the value proposition
- Providing higher-quality product content
- Improving traffic relevance

### 2. Traffic Performance Analysis

<br>

<p align="center">
  <img width="485" height="213" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6a6747e6-b216-4d05-a45e-448caca18c52" />
</p>

Traffic showed volatility throughout the January–September 2025 period, peaking in March and dropping sharply in April before rebounding in May–June. It dipped again in July and then stabilized at a moderate level through August–September.

The growth in the first quarter, followed by a V-shaped correction, was likely driven by period-specific factors, such as temporary campaigns or promotional activities. This indicates that traffic spikes do not necessarily reflect stable acquisition performance, highlighting the need for sustained campaigns and more consistent traffic sources.

<br>
<p align="center">
  <img width="482" height="210" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/06e4dd3f-38fb-43b2-be1a-8edb1778c65a" />
</p>

Social Media drives the largest share of traffic (37.5%), but its conversion rate (13.3%) lags behind Direct (15.5%) and Email (15.2%).

From a marketing efficiency standpoint, Social Media is effective for reach but attracts less purchase-ready users, whereas Direct and Email deliver higher-quality despite lower volumes.

### 3. Device Performance Analysis

<br>

<p align="center">
  <img width="214" height="260" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/94ff4672-3c28-4d0c-af1d-2325ed024064" />
</p>

Mobile leads with 2,999 sessions (53%), nearly 1.7× Desktop and 3.3× Tablet. With over half of traffic coming from mobile, optimizing its stability, speed, and navigation offers the highest potential ROI for boosting overall conversions.

<p align="center">
  <img width="264" height="257" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/0b11f893-d938-43fb-b52c-478e9e2ab17e" />
</p>

Session duration correlates with cart intensity: Desktop (196s, ~4 items) and Mobile (181s, ~4 items) show strong engagement and purchase intent. While Tablet(122s, ~2 items), significantly shorter interaction time and lower cart activity, indicating friction in the shopping experience and opportunities for optimization.

<p align="center">
  <img width="525" height="308" alt="Image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ed5b59ff-89de-47c6-b665-d5a1ab268990" />

Tablet consistently underperforms:

- Conversion rate 6.7% (less than half of Desktop and Mobile)
- Shorter session duration
- Lower cart intensity

A UX audit on Tablet devices, focusing on layout, responsiveness, and navigation is needed to reduce friction and improve engagement and conversions.


<br>
<h1 align="center">Strategic Recommendations</h1>
<br>

**1. Optimize Product Page → Add to Cart Conversion** <br>
&emsp;Improve the transition from Product Page to Add to Cart by clearer CTAs and value propositions, enhancing product visuals, providing pricing transparency and social proof, and strengthening overall messaging. Use A/B testing to optimize key page elements that drive cart commitment and reduce drop-offs.

**2. Enhance Traffic Quality Strategy** <br>
&emsp;Improve marketing efficiency by attracting higher-intent traffic. Refocus Social Media targeting and messaging to attract higher purchase-intent users while scaling Email and Direct campaigns due to their higher conversion efficiency.

**3. Mobile Experience Optimization** <br>
&emsp;Maximize conversion potential on Mobile, which drives the majority of traffic, by improving loading speed, simplifying navigation and product discovery, and ensuring a smooth checkout flow.  Clear CTAs, responsive design, and fast performance can reduce friction and convert high mobile traffic into more purchases.

**4. Tablet UX Audit & Optimization** <br>
&emsp;Conduct a comprehensive UX review for Tablet devices, addressing layout, responsiveness, and navigation friction. Align the Tablet experience more closely with Desktop and Mobile benchmarks.
